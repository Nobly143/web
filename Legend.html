Nelson Mandela

Former president of South Africa, known for leading the resistance to South Africa's policy of Aparthied in the 20th century.

Herioc Timeline:
  1. Nelson Mandela was the president of South Africa from 1994-1999.
  2. He was the first black president of South Africa.
  3. First president to be elected in a fully representative election
  4. Nelson Mandela's government focused on destroying the Apartheid government in the country, which had focused on racial segregation enforced by the law.
  5. In school, Mandela studied law and became one of South Africa's first black lawyers.
  6. In the 1950s, he was elected leader of the youth wing of the African National Congress (ANC) liberation movement
  7. Mandela was imprisoned from 1962-1990 for treason and conspiracy against the government. He was sentenced to life in prison, but was released early when the ANC became legal again.
  8. While he was in prison, Mandela was a symbol to rally behind for the oppressed in South Africa who were fighting for rights
  9. Mandela received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 for peacefully destroying the Apartheid regime and laying the foundation for democracy. In addition to the Nobel Piece Prize, he won over 250 other awards.
  10. Mandela is considered the father of Modern South Africa. He was instrumental in tearing down the oppressive government and installing democracy
Mandela was a controversial figure for much of his life. Although critics on the right denounced him as a communist terrorist and those on the far left deemed him too eager to negotiate and reconcile with apartheid's supporters, he gained international acclaim for his activism. Globally regarded as an icon of democracy and social justice, he received more than 250 honours, including the Nobel Peace Prize. He is held in deep respect within South Africa, where he is often referred to by his Thembu clan name, Madiba, and described as the "Father of the Nation".
